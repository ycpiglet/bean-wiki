Development is not just what happens after first crack

In practice, many refer to the period from the start of first crack to discharge as the development stage. While useful for recording and communication, the reactions that create flavor occur continuously throughout the entire roasting process.

Therefore, rather than using development time or ratio as a single quality target value, you need to read the charge temperature, energy application, rate of change, and discharge state together.

Signals to record together

The temperature displayed by the machine is influenced by sensor position and response speed. Don't just look at the curve—recording color, aroma, sound, exhaust condition, and physical changes together makes batch-to-batch comparison more accurate.

  • Batch weight and green bean density and moisture content
  • Timing of gas or electrical input changes
  • Exhaust and drum speed
  • Yellow point, first crack, and discharge timing
  • Weight loss ratio, color, and cupping results

The answer to your profile is confirmed in the cup

A good profile is not a beautiful-looking curve but a profile that consistently produces the intended flavor. Cupping under the same conditions and connecting roasting records to results creates meaningful benchmarks.
