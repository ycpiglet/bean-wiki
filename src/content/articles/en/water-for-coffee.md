## Composition Matters More Than a Single Hardness Number {#hardness}

In the context of coffee, hardness is primarily related to polyvalent cations like calcium and magnesium. The type and concentration of ions can have different effects on the extraction of coffee components and on sensory impression.

## Alkalinity Buffers Acidity {#alkalinity}

Alkalinity represents the water's ability to neutralize acid. High alkalinity can make acidity feel duller, while excessively low alkalinity can make it feel sharp and unstable.

Since hardness and alkalinity are not the same concept, it is difficult to judge extraction water based on a single total dissolved solids (TDS) value.

## Measure, Record, and Verify with Taste {#practice}

Periodically measure raw water and water after filtration, and record filter replacement timing together with recipes and sensory evaluation results. You must consider both water quality for equipment protection and water quality for desired flavor.
