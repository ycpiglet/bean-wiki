Create Common Conditions for Comparison

Cupping is a sensory evaluation method used to compare coffee during production, purchasing, roasting, and quality control processes. By controlling the vessel, ratio, grind, water, time, and temperature, you can focus on the differences in the samples themselves.

Observe from Hot to Cool

As temperature decreases, the volatility of aromas and the perception of taste change. Rather than one strong impression at a single point in time, examine how aroma, aftertaste, acidity, sweetness, texture, and balance change across different temperature ranges.

  • Confirm intensity and clarity before identifying specific aroma types.
  • Distinguish between subjective judgment (good or bad) and observed characteristics when recording.
  • Through repeat cups, verify uniformity within the sample and identify defects.

Calibration Comes Before Scores

Sensory evaluation is measurement using human senses. It is important for evaluators to align their terminology and intensity standards, and to discuss the basis for different judgments through calibration.

Beginners should practice starting with easy-to-compare axes like acidity intensity, sweetness persistence, and texture weight, rather than trying to pinpoint specific aroma names.
