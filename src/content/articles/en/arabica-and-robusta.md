## Two Different Species {#species}

The two most commercially cultivated coffees are Arabica and Canephora (commonly called Robusta). The two species differ genetically and show differences in their growing environment, resilience to diseases, and chemical composition.

## Differences in Growing Environment {#growing}

Arabica generally thrives in cooler highland regions but is relatively sensitive to diseases. Robusta tolerates lower altitudes and warmer climates well and tends to have higher yields.

- Arabica: prefers highlands, tends toward delicate flavors
- Robusta: hardy in lowlands and high heat, tends toward higher caffeine content
- Quality cannot be determined by altitude alone.

## Flavor Is Not Determined by Species Alone {#flavor}

While species characteristics set the broad direction of flavor, the final cup is determined together by all stages from variety, cultivation management, processing and roasting, to extraction. Rather than dividing good from bad based on the species name alone, the entire process must be considered together.
