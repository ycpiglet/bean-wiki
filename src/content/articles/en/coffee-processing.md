## Understanding the Layers of the Cherry Reveals Processing {#layers}

The coffee seed is surrounded by parchment, mucilage, pulp, and skin. Processing methods can be distinguished by when and how these layers are removed, and how the seed is dried.

## Three Representative Approaches {#methods}

The terminology and detailed methods may vary by country and producer. Even under the same name, results can differ significantly depending on fermentation time, water usage, drying environment, and sorting level.

- Washed: Pulp is removed, mucilage is broken down and removed, and dried in a parchment state
- Natural: The entire cherry is dried, then the dried skin and pulp are removed
- Honey/Pulped Natural: Pulp is removed, and some or all of the mucilage is retained and dried

## Quality is Determined by Process Management {#quality}

Uniform ripeness, sanitary fermentation, drying speed, moisture uniformity, and storage conditions have a major impact on quality and consistency. New fermentation techniques should also be evaluated based on process variables, reproducibility, and sensory results rather than just their names.
