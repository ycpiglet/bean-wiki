## Species and Variety Are Different Levels of Classification {#species-vs-variety}

Species (species) is a large category distinguished biologically, like Arabica or Canephora. Variety (variety) refers to cultivated lines within one species that have slightly different genetic characteristics.

In other words, if 'Arabica' is the species, then 'Typica' or 'Bourbon' within it are varieties. Most names encountered in geographic information or on coffee bags are distinctions at this variety level.

## Representative Varieties You Commonly Meet {#examples}

Varieties show differences not only in flavor potential but also in cultivation characteristics such as yield and disease resistance.

- Typica: An old classic lineage with a tendency toward delicate flavors, but relatively low yield
- Bourbon: A lineage descended from Typica; often praised for good sweetness and balance
- Geisha (Gesha): A variety that stands out for its floral aromas and distinctive flavors
- Caturra · Catuaí: Lineages improved for productivity and cultivation convenience

## You Cannot Determine the Cup from Variety Name Alone {#caution}

While variety greatly influences the direction and potential of flavor, the actual taste in your cup is determined together by all stages from cultivation environment, processing, roasting, to extraction. It is difficult to confirm quality based solely on the fact that it is a famous variety.
